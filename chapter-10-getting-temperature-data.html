8 Chapter 10 — Getting temperature data

In this chapter, we learn how to obtain and prepare historical temperature data for a chosen location, using the Lake Constance region as an example.

8.1 Selecting a study location and nearby weather stations

Choose a location of interest and find the 25 closest weather stations using the handle_gsod function

Choice of study region:

  • In general, I wanted to use the Lake Constance region because it is a major fruit production area in Germany
  • Initially, I planned to use the same location, Gottmadingen (Baden-Württemberg), as in my bachelor’s thesis
  • I quickly realized that there is no nearby weather station for Gottmadingen
  • I then considered Consatnce, but the available weather data only extend until 2003
  • Another option was the weather station in Schaffhausen, which has good data coverage
  • However, I found the milder Lake Constance climate more interesting for the analysis
  • The next weather station with nearly complete data is Güttingen (Thurgau), Switzerland, which is also located directly on Lake Constance

Climatic and agricultural relevance of the region:

  • The Lake Constance region typically has mild, warm autumns, which are very favorable for fruit production and especially viticulture
  • Large day–night temperature differences are a key factor that support optimal fruit ripening
  • Güttingen hosts an agricultural experimental farm, the Schul- und Versuchsbetrieb in Güttingen, which is actively used for research in fruit and berry cultivation
  • At this site, more than 10 fruit and berry species and over 100 different varieties are cultivated
  • The Swiss canton of Thurgau is the largest fruit-growing region in Switzerland, with a gross income of approximately 80 million Swiss francs from fruit production
  • Like most regions, Thurgau is affected by climate change, with meteorological records dating back to 1864 showing clear signals of climatic change
  • Extreme weather events have become more frequent, including heatwaves (e.g. 2018) and heavy rainfall events (e.g. 2021)
  • Since 1864, mean temperatures in Thurgau have increased by about 1.9 °C.
  • Due to its importance for fruit production and its strong exposure to climate change, Güttingen is well suited for further studies in this module
