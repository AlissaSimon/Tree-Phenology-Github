2 Chapter 3 — Tree Dormancy

This chapter focuses on understanding dormancy in temperate fruit trees.

2.1 Exercise 1

Put yourself in the place of a breeder who wants to calculate the temperature requirements of a newly released cultivar. Which method will you use to calculate the chilling and forcing periods? Please justify your answer

-\> Statistical method

-   experimental method is time consuming and labor intensive, as you need to repeatedly collect buds in the field, place them in the grow chambers an observe the bud growth → risk for a new cultivar with unknown behavior
-   statistical method analyzes field data together with temperature records
-   by applying correlation or regression approaches, you can identify
    -   periods with positive significant coefficients → chilling phases
    -   periods with negative coefficients → forcing phase
-   statistical method provides an objective, data-driven estimation of temperature requirements, can be updated with new years of data and allows to model cultivar response under climate-change scenarios

2.2 Exercise 2

Which are the advantages (2) of the BBCH scale compared with earlier scales?

-   Universality and standardization
    -   BBCH scale provides a single coding system that can describe the growth stages of most crops
    -   earlier scales e.g. Fleckinger 1948, Baglioni 1953) were species-specific and not easily comparable between crops
    -   easily recognizable field traits with a two-digit numeric code
-   Detail and clarity
    -   BBCH distinguishes principal (0–9) and secondary growth stages, allowing a precise description of development
    -   Earlier systems had only a few broad stages and missed intermediate steps
